About

I am currently an assistant professor in the School of Computing and Information Sciences at Saint Francis University, where I also serve as the Programme Leader for the Higher Diploma in Crime and Security Science. I received my B.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science from City University of Hong Kong under the supervision of Prof. Chee Wei Tan, where I was awarded the CityU Outstanding Academic Performance Award for my Ph.D. studies. I also previously served as a Machine Learning Center Researcher at Tencent AI Lab.

My research interests span data science and artificial intelligence, with a focus on healthcare and education applications. Specific research projects include the optimization of large language models and their applications in combating misinformation during crises through automated fact-checking for public health informatics, enhancing autonomous learning in educational systems, and analyzing academic publishing using network science. My Erdös number is 3. More details about my research and publications can be found on my Google Scholar profile page, research statement, and CV.
